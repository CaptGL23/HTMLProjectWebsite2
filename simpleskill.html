10/18/20, Daniel Litten, Unit 4: How To Make Pastelillos

This is a family recipe. It is an authentic Puerto Rican dish that has been passed onto myself from my mother. She learned how to make it from my grandmother and so on and so forth. Making pastelillos is quite simple, but is very time consuming. I usually make 30 of them for my family alone because we all love them. I also usually make a side dish of spanish rice, which I also use a family recipe for. Here I will only be explaining how to make pastelillos.

Ingredients

Ingredient Amount
Italian Seasoning 1 tsp
Parsley Flakes 1 tsp
Oregano 1 tsp
Adobo 1 tsp
Goya Sazon 2 packets
Basil 1 tsp
Garlic Powder 1 tsp
Racaito 1-2 tbsp
Minced Garlic 1-2 tbsp
Sofrito 1-2 tbsp
Ground Beef 1 lb.
Potato 1 medium sized
Goya Discos 3 packs of 10

Preparation

  • Ensure you have all ingredients.
  • Peal, rinse, then dice potato into small pieces.
  • Place diced potatos into small pan with water, covering the potatos, and place onto stove.
  • Ensure Goya Discos are thawed.
  • Preheat your fryer or oil to 360-375 degrees.

Cooking Instructions

  1. Cook potatos over medium heat until al dente. DO NOT OVER COOK!
  2. While potatos are cooking, cook your ground beef over medium heat.
  3. When the ground beef begins to brown, add and stir in all of your seasonings; Italian Seasoning, Parsley Flakes, Oregano, Adobo, Basil, Garlic Powder, Racaito, Minced Garlic, and Sofrito (DO NOT ADD THE SAZON YET).
  4. Once the beef has browned, add in your Sazon and cook on low until potatos are done and drained (Again, ensure the you do not cook the potatos too long or else they will break apart when you add them to beef).
  5. Add your diced potato to the meat and stir them in, cooking on low.
  6. Remove your meat/potatos from heat and get your Goya Discos open and ready.
  7. Add a generous amount of meat/potatos to your disco.
  8. Fold the disco in half and use a fork to press the edges closed (I go over the edges twice, to ensure they are closed, so that the discos do not open while cooking).
  9. Put your discos with meat/potatos into your preheated oil and cook on both sides until both sides are golden brown.
  10. And there you have it, Puerto Rican Pastelillos!!!!
